THE DEFINITION OF SOLDIER PILE

A soldier pile is a cylindrical column or a row of cast-in-situ bored piles designed to resist lateral earth pressure on retaining walls. These piles are typically embedded vertically in the ground and provide structural support to prevent soil movement.

WORK METHOD

The work method for soldier piles is similar to that of bored piles, with the main differences being their function and placement. Soldier piles are typically installed in a row, spaced 1-2 meters apart, depending on the design. They serve to resist lateral earth pressure on retaining walls, while bored piles are more commonly used as foundation elements. The use of soldier piles is more specific to soil retention contexts, whereas bored piles have a broader range of applications in construction.

THE ADVANTAGES OF SOLDIER PILE

1

Effective Lateral Support

Soldier piles provide strong resistance against lateral earth pressures, making them ideal for retaining wall systems.

2

Flexible Design

Soldier piles can be easily adapted to various soil conditions and design requirements, allowing for customized solutions.

3

Fast Installation

Soldier piles can be installed relatively quickly, allowing for faster project timelines.

APPLICATION SOLDIER PILE

Retaining Walls

Commonly used in the construction of retaining walls to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Excavation Support

Used to support excavations in urban areas where deep digging is required.

Temporary Shoring

Often employed as temporary support during construction activities to protect adjacent structures.

Slope Stabilization

Used to stabilize slopes in areas prone to landslides or erosion.

Foundation Support

In some cases, soldier piles can be utilized as part of the foundation system for structures built on challenging soil conditions.
