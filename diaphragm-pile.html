THE DEFINITION OF DIAPHRAGM WALL

A diaphragm wall is a type of reinforced concrete wall that is constructed in deep excavations to provide support and stability to the surrounding soil. It is typically formed by using a continuous series of panels, which are constructed in situ by excavating the soil and then pouring concrete into the created trench. Diaphragm walls are used to retain soil and groundwater, providing lateral support for excavations and forming a barrier against water ingress.

WORK METHOD

1. Site Preparation

Clear the site and set up necessary access roads, safety measures, and utilities.

2. Excavation

Use an appropriate excavation method (such as a hydraulic grab or trenching machine) to create a trench for the diaphragm wall. The depth and width depend on design requirements.

3. Polymer Slurry Application

Use polymer slurry to stabilize the trench walls and prevent water ingress during the construction process.

4. CWS with Rubber Water Stop Application

Position the rubber water stop correctly at the joint locations. Make sure it is properly secured without any folds or creases.

5. Rebar Cage Placement

After excavation, place the rebar cages into the trench according to the design specifications.

6. Concrete the panel

After lowering all the rebar cages, install the tremie pipes. Pour concrete into the trench to form the diaphragm wall. Ensure proper vibration and consolidation to avoid voids.

7. Curing and Backfilling

Allow the concrete to cure adequately to achieve the desired strength. After the diaphragm wall is constructed and cured, backfill any required areas.

THE ADVANTAGES OF DIAPHRAGM WALL

1

High Structural Integrity

Diaphragm walls provide robust lateral support and can withstand significant soil and water pressures.

2

Waterproofing

Effective in preventing water ingress, making them suitable for underground constructions.

3

Versatility

Can be used in a variety of soil conditions and can be adapted to different project requirements.

4

Space Efficiency

The vertical nature of diaphragm walls allows for optimal use of space in urban environments.

5

Reduced Ground Movement

Minimizes disturbance to surrounding soil and structures during installation.

APPLICATION DIAPHRAGM WALL

Deep Excavations in Limited Space

Commonly used to support deep excavations in urban areas where space is limited.

Basements and Underground Structures

Employed in the construction of basements, parking garages, and underground utilities.

Retention of Soil and Groundwater

Used to retain soil and control groundwater levels in various construction projects.

Tunneling Projects

Can serve as temporary or permanent support in tunneling applications.

Flood Protection

Utilized in flood defense structures to retain soil and water in vulnerable areas.
